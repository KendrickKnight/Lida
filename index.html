Research Focus

Stem cell biology

Stem cells are undifferentiated cells of the body, able to differentiate into functional cells as required or guided. discovery of different stem cells and their potential therapy applications in recent years, stem cell therapy has become a very promising and advanced scientific research topic. There are many factors regulating the fate of a stem cell which can be exploited to reach the desired differentiation. Using genetic, epigenetic and microenvironmental modulations, our studies have shown promising prospects for treatment using stem cells.

Development biology

Developmental biology is the study of interacting processes generating an organism’s heterogeneous shapes, size, and structural features that arise throughout a life cycle. It represents an exemplary area of contemporary experimental biology that focuses on phenomena for understanding evolution, the theme of reductionism in genetic explanations, and stem cell biology. Developmental biology displays a rich array of material and conceptual practices that can be analyzed to better understand the scientific reasoning exhibited in experimental life science. Studying regulating sequences such as enhancer cluster using CRISPR/Cas9-mediated deletion approach we have concluded that clusters of enhancers acting on a common target gene act in a partially redundant manner to fine tune transcriptional output of their target genes.

Transcriptional regulations

In molecular biology and genetics, transcriptional regulation is the means by which a cell regulates the conversion of DNA to RNA (transcription), thereby orchestrating gene activity. Transcriptional regulation is a critical biological process that allows the cell or an organism to respond to a variety of intra- and extra-cellular signals, to define cell identity during development, to maintain it throughout its lifetime, and to coordinate cellular activity. Transcriptional regulation consists of a huge number of molecules establishing larger networks and occurring through multiple temporal and functional steps that range from specific DNA-protein interactions to the recruitment and assembly of nucleoprotein complexes. In this regard, using various knock down mechanisms against transcription factors in mouse embryonic stem cells, we were able to find novel functions attributed to pluripotency TFs.
